Budget Jambalaya: Stretching Andouille Across Eight Servings

A single ring of andouille sausage, diced small, gives every bite of rice a hit of smoke. Bulk long-grain rice beats pre-seasoned boxed mixes on this audit — the markup on the box isn't worth it.

INGREDIENT UNIT LEDGER

SERVINGS: 8
INGREDIENT ITEM QTY UNIT COST
Andouille Sausage, Diced 1 ring $4.50
Long-Grain Rice (Bulk) 3 cups $0.60
Chicken Broth 5 cups $0.90
Bell Pepper, Diced 1 pepper $0.90
Celery, Diced 2 stalks $0.40
Diced Tomatoes (Can) 1 can (14.5oz) $0.90
Cajun Seasoning 2 tbsp $0.30
ESTIMATED TOTAL MATRIX: $8.50
COST PER SERVING: $1.06

Preparation Protocol

  1. Render the Sausage: Brown diced andouille in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat until fat renders, about 5 minutes.
  2. Build the Trinity: Add bell pepper and celery to the pot and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add Rice and Liquid: Stir in rice, diced tomatoes, broth, and Cajun seasoning.
  4. Simmer: Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer on low for 20-25 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
