Sheet Pan Seasonal Audit: Buying Whatever's Cheapest That Week

Rather than chasing a fixed recipe, this audit builds a template for pricing whatever root vegetable is cheapest per pound that week and roasting it the same way — the method stays fixed, the produce line item floats.

INGREDIENT UNIT LEDGER

SERVINGS: 4
INGREDIENT ITEM QTY UNIT COST
Seasonal Root Vegetable (Cheapest Per Lb) 2 lb $1.60
Olive Oil 2 tbsp $0.36
Salt 1 tsp $0.02
Black Pepper 0.5 tsp $0.01
Dried Rosemary or Thyme 1 tsp $0.08
ESTIMATED TOTAL MATRIX: $2.08
COST PER SERVING: $0.52

Preparation Protocol

  1. Prep the Vegetable: Wash and chop whichever root vegetable is cheapest that week into even, 1-inch pieces.
  2. Season: Toss chopped vegetable with olive oil, salt, pepper, and dried herbs on a sheet pan.
  3. Roast: Roast at 425°F in a single layer, flipping halfway, until deeply caramelized and fork-tender.
  4. Serve: Serve hot as a side or over grains for a full meal.
