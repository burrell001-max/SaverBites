AUDIT ID: POT_BEEF_BARLEY_05

Beef & Barley Stew Blueprint

A volume expansion play via grain hydration. Instead of scaling up expensive beef metrics, we leverage pearled barley as a high-density asset that absorbs our seasoned broth while introducing robust chew and satiety indexes to the batch.

1. Component Unit Ledger

INGREDIENT COMPONENT QUANTITY USED UNIT COST MATRIX TOTAL COST
Beef Chuck / Stew Meat Assets 300g $0.014 / g $4.20

Mirepoix Base (Onions, Carrots, Celery) 350g combined $0.002 / g $0.70
Tomato Paste Concentrate & Soy Sauce 30g / 15ml Pantry Stock $0.45
Beef Bouillon Base & Aromatics 2 Cubes / Thyme Negligible $0.20

2. Optimization Steps

01 / Fond Generation: Pat your beef cubes completely dry. Sear them in a hot pot with vegetable oil until dark brown crusts form on the edges. Remove the beef pieces, leaving the deep brown caramelized sugar fractions (fond) on the bottom of the pot to season the upcoming vegetable base.

02 / Aromatic Integration: Drop your chopped vegetables into the pot to sweat. Add the tomato paste and raw pearled barley directly to the hot fat matrix, stirring for 2 minutes. Toasting the barley kernels slightly before adding liquid deepens the overall flavor profile.

03 / Long-Form Hydro-Softening: Return the beef pieces to the pot, pour in the bouillon water and soy sauce, and scrape the bottom clean. Cover with a tight lid, bring down to a steady low simmer, and allow the mix to process for 1 hour. The barley will swell to three times its size, naturally turning the broth into a rich, velvety gravy.
