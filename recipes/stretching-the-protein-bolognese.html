Stretching the Protein: Optimizing 1/2 Pound of Beef Across a Gallon of Bolognese

You don't need pounds of meat for a satisfying meat sauce. Finely minced mirepoix matches the texture of ground beef, tripling sauce volume while keeping a high-end texture.

INGREDIENT UNIT LEDGER

SERVINGS: 6
INGREDIENT ITEM QTY UNIT COST
Ground Beef (80/20) 0.5 lb $2.25
Carrots, Finely Minced 2 carrots $0.50
Celery Stalks, Finely Minced 2 stalks $0.40
Yellow Onion, Finely Minced 1 onion $0.30
Crushed Tomatoes (Can) 1 can (28oz) $1.30
Rigatoni or Tagliatelle 16 oz $1.28
ESTIMATED TOTAL MATRIX: $6.03
COST PER SERVING: $1.01

Preparation Protocol

  1. Build the Mirepoix: Finely mince carrots, celery, and onion until the pieces are roughly the size of ground beef crumbles.
  2. Brown the Beef: Brown ground beef in a heavy pot over medium-high heat, breaking it into small pieces, about 6 minutes.
  3. Sweat the Vegetables: Add the minced mirepoix to the beef and cook until softened, about 8 minutes.
  4. Simmer: Stir in crushed tomatoes and simmer uncovered for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thick and rich.
  5. Serve: Toss with freshly cooked pasta and serve immediately.
