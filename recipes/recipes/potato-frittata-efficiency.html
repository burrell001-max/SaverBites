Potato Frittata Matrix

A framework for optimizing egg-based yields. By interlocking thinly sliced tuber starch channels directly with fluid egg lipids, we form a dense structural cake that doubles the volume threshold of a standard scramble at a fraction of the unit cost.

1. Component Unit Ledger

Russet Potatoes (Thinly Sliced) 400g (2 Medium) $0.0016 / g $0.64
Yellow Onion (Halved & Sliced) 150g (1 Medium) $0.0014 / g $0.21
Vegetable Cooking Oil 30ml $0.003 / ml $0.09
Sea Salt & Fine Black Pepper 8g Pantry Stock $0.05

2. Optimization Steps

01 / Starch Foundation Layering: Scrub and slice potatoes into thin 1/8-inch disks. Heat oil in a non-stick skillet and layer the potatoes and onions. Fry over medium heat for 12 minutes, turning them gently so they soften and lightly brown without completely breaking apart.

02 / Egg Matrix Injection: Crack eggs into a bowl, whisk thoroughly with salt and pepper, and pour evenly over the pan potato assembly. Tap the pan against the burner so the raw egg runs down through the cracks, creating a solid, interconnected structure.

03 / Thermal Set Completion: Drop the burner heat to its absolute lowest threshold, cover with a flat lid, and let steam for 10 minutes. If your pan is oven-safe, you can slide it under a broiler for 2 minutes to brown the top. Slice clean wedge segments directly from the skillet matrix.
