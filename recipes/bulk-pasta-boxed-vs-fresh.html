Bulk Pasta Audits: Gram-for-Gram Cost Analysis of Boxed vs. Fresh

Is homemade pasta actually cheaper, or are you paying with your time? We weigh bulk semolina flour against standard boxed pasta on a true cost-per-ounce basis.

INGREDIENT UNIT LEDGER

SERVINGS: 4
INGREDIENT ITEM QTY UNIT COST
Semolina Flour (Bulk Bag) 10 oz $0.60
Large Eggs 3 large $0.75
Olive Oil 1 tbsp $0.18
Salt 1 tsp $0.02
ESTIMATED TOTAL MATRIX: $1.55
COST PER SERVING: $0.39

Preparation Protocol

  1. Mound and Well: Pour semolina onto a work surface and form a well in the center. Crack eggs and oil into the well.
  2. Knead: Gradually incorporate flour into the eggs with a fork, then knead by hand for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  3. Rest: Wrap dough in plastic and rest at room temperature to relax the gluten.
  4. Roll and Cut: Roll dough thin with a pasta machine or rolling pin, then cut into the shape of your choice.
  5. Cook: Boil fresh pasta in salted water for just 2-3 minutes, since fresh sheets cook far faster than boxed dry pasta.
